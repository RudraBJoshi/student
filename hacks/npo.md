On May 22th, 2026, I had the opportunity to participate to pitch to a nonprofit panel at the CTE Career Fair. The panel was organized by the Career and Technical Education (CTE) and my computer science teacher, Mr. Mortensen. It featured representatives from various industries. The goal of the panel was to provide students feedback on their pitches and to help them improve their communication skills. I was one of the students who pitched to the panel, and I found the experience to be very valuable. 

The nonprofit I plan to pitch the idea to is the Unified Esports League. The Unified Esports League is a nonprofit organization that promotes inclusivity and diversity in the esports industry. They provide opportunities for individuals with disabilities to participate in esports competitions and events. My pitch was focused on how I could contribute to the organization by creating a website that would help the community navigate and connect with each other. I wanted to create a platform where members of the Unified Esports League could share their experiences, find resources, and connect with others who share their interests withoout excluding the IDD community.

Me and my team worked hard to prepare for the pitch. We researched the organization and its mission, and we developed a website that we believed would be a valuable resource for the community. We also practiced our pitch multiple times to ensure that we were able to effectively communicate our ideas to the panel.

Our panalists were:
- Rajeev Pal, Vice President of Qualcomm
- Scott Vandervort, Software Engineer from CTE
- and Jeffrey Morales, another Software Engineer from CTE

The panel provided us with valuable feedback on our pitch. They praised our idea and the work we had done to prepare for the pitch. They also provided us with constructive criticism on how we could improve our pitch and make it more effective. For example, they suggested new features for the website and pointed out a couple of bugs. Overall we recieved a lot of positive feedback and encouragement from the panel, which was very motivating for us.

Participating in the pitching to the panel was a great experience for me. It allowed me to practice my communication skills and receive feedback from industry professionals. It also gave me the opportunity to contribute to a cause that I am passionate about, which is promoting inclusivity and diversity in the esports industry. I am grateful for the opportunity to participate in this panel and I look forward to continuing to work on my pitch and contribute to the Unified Esports League in the future.
